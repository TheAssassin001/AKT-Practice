Practice Question: Cardiology

Which of the following is the most appropriate next step in the management of a patient presenting with new-onset atrial fibrillation?
Correct answer: D. Begin anticoagulation
Explanation: This is a dummy explanation. In patients with new-onset atrial fibrillation, anticoagulation is often indicated to reduce the risk of stroke, depending on risk assessment.
